Try one now, free

Here's one of the tests from the book. It reads your ankle, and a stiff ankle is behind more sore knees than most people would ever guess. Takes a minute, and you can do it against any wall in the house.

Knee to wall

Stand facing a wall, one foot forward with the toes right up against it. Keep that front heel pressed flat on the floor, then bend the knee forward to touch the wall. Easy at first. Now shuffle the foot back a little and try again, heel still flat, knee still reaching. Keep edging back until the knee will only just touch with the heel staying down. That gap between your toes and the wall is your limit.

What you're after is about a hand's width. Get your toes that far back with the knee still touching and the heel flat, and your ankle's got the give it needs.

If your heel popped up early, or the foot turned out to cheat a bit more, that ankle's run out of give. And a stiff ankle downstairs makes the knee above it do work it was never meant to. That's exactly the kind of thing this book teaches you to spot, and put right.
