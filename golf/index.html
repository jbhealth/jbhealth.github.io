Where your distance went

It's rarely the swing, it's the body under it

Past 50, the limit usually isn't your technique, it's the body under it: the turn shortens, the back tightens by the ninth, the closing holes feel nothing like the first. That's rarely age alone, more often physical limits you can train to improve.

A good swing, and the distance and accuracy it brings, come from four things working together: coordination, so the swing fires in the right order; mobility, for a full turn that loads behind the ball; stability, a solid base so no power leaks away; and strength, to drive it through impact. Each one fades a little over time, and each one can be rebuilt. Do that, and the yards and accuracy return.

What I train

A round asks three things of your body

  • Rotation that frees the swing. A fuller, easier turn through the hips and mid-back: more coil, less strain, and the clubhead speed that adds distance.
  • Mobility and control. Hips, shoulders, mid-back and ankles moving freely and under control, so the joints aren't taking load they shouldn't and your ball-striking stays consistent.
  • Stamina for 18. Conditioning so holes 16 to 18 feel like 1 to 3, your swing as sharp on the last tee as the first.
Less pain

Fix the cause, not just the round-day ache

If your back, shoulder or knees flare during a round or stiffen the next morning, the right training usually fixes it at the source: a hip that won't rotate, a locked mid-back, a shoulder short on range.

Common questions

  • Can golf fitness add distance? Yes. Most lost distance after 50 comes from a restricted turn and weak rotation, not technique. Train the turn, hip and mid-back mobility and lower-body power and you restore the clubhead speed that distance comes from.
  • Will it help my consistency? A stable, mobile body repeats its swing more easily. Better control through the hips, core and shoulders means a more repeatable strike, and fewer breakdowns over the closing holes.
  • Can I train around back, knee or shoulder pain? Yes. The programming is built around old injuries and restrictions, working the cause rather than the round-day ache, so you play more comfortably.
  • Do I need a gym? No. Every programme is tailored to the kit you have, from a full gym to a few dumbbells and a band at home, and delivered to your own programme page on your phone.
